Stovetop Hot Cocoa

Description

Hot Cocoa can most definitely knock the cold out of our Autumn and Winter months! It's a staple that is made a lot in my household. Originally from Mexico, people have been enjoying drinking chocolate since around 500 bc.
The Mayans made a far different version than the one we enjoy, using ground up cocoa seeds mixed with water, cornmeal, chili peppers, and even other ingredients.
We all know hot chocolate as the warm, rich drink we enjoy on a cold night by the fire, or after engaging in winter activities such as ice skating and skiing. Hopefully your family can enjoy this simple recipe as well as mine does!

Ingredients

  • 3/4 Cup of White Sugar (or To Taste)

  • 1/3 Cup Unsweetened Cocoa Powder

  • 1 Pinch of Salt

  • 1/3 Cup of Boiling Water

  • 3 1/2 Cups of Milk

  • 3/4 Tablespoon of Vanilla Extract

  • 1/2 Cup of Half and Half Cream

Steps

  1. Combine sugar, cocoa powder, and salt in a saucepan. Add boiling water and whisk until smooth. Bring mixture to a simmer, stirring continuously to prevent scorching, and cook for 2 minutes.


  2. Stir in Milk and heat until very hot but NOT boiling
  3. Remove from heat and add Vanilla Extract

  4. Add cream to each mug to help cool cocoa to drinking temperature then divide hot cocoa between 4 mugs.

  5. Serve and Enjoy!
