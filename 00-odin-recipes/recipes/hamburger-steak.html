Hamburger Steak with Onions and Gravy

Description

Tasty hamburger "steaks" smothered in gravy and onions. Serve with hot rice or potatoes for an easy-to-make dinner classic. It's a great way to dress up a pound of ground beef and you probably have all the ingredients on hand! I love it over homemade mashed potatoes, and the gravy really helps. It keeps the family happy and full as the days get colder. Hamburger steak is similar to Salisbury steak, which is also made with ground beef. The main difference is that hamburger steak is made with ground beef, while chopped steak is made with ground sirloin. Hamburger steak originated from German sailors who brought Russian steak tartare back to Hamburg, Germany. They originally called it “tartare steak” and later “Hamburg steak”. In the early 20th century, vendors began selling Hamburg steak as a sandwich between bread, which is considered the origin of the hamburger.

Ingredients

  • One Pound Ground Beef

  • 1/4 Cup of Breadcrumbs

  • 1 Egg

  • 1 Teaspoon Worcestershire Sauce

  • Spices

    • You will need 1/2 teaspoon each of Seasoned Salt, Onion Powder, and Garlic Powder. 1/8 teaspoon of black pepper should be used as well. To taste, of course.

  • 1 Teaspoon Vegitable Oil

  • 1 Cup Thinly Sliced Onion

  • 1 Tablespoons of All-Purpose Flour

  • 1 Cup of Beef Broth

  • 1 Tablespoon of Cooking Sherry

Difficulty Spike!

This recipe is a tad more difficult than the chili, so it will contain a bit more information as well as more pictures of the process. Believe in yourself, you can make this with just a bit of work!

Steps

  1. Gather All Ingredients

  2. Mix ground beef, bread crumbs, egg, Worcestershire sauce, salt, onion powder, garlic powder, and pepper together in a large bowl until combined.
    Form into 8 balls and flatten into patties.

  3. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add patties and onion; fry until patties are nicely browned, about 4 minutes per side. Transfer beef patties to a plate, and keep warm.

  4. Sprinkle flour over onions and drippings in the skillet. Stir in flour with a fork, scraping bits of beef off of the bottom of the skillet as you stir. Gradually mix in beef broth and sherry.
    Season with seasoned salt. Simmer and stir over medium-low heat until gravy thickens, about 5 minutes.

  5. Reduce heat to low, return patties to the gravy, cover, and simmer until cooked through, about 15 minutes.

  6. Serve and Enjoy!

    On a bed of Mashed Potatoes or even some whire rice, It'll fill you up and please the family!
